# BrightTV Viewership Analytics

**Customer Value Management — Growth Strategy Presentation**
Q1 2016 | January – March | 10,000 Sessions Analysed
*Confidential — BrightTV CVM Team*

---

## Overview

This project presents a data-driven analysis of BrightTV's subscriber viewership behaviour for Q1 2016. It covers session activity, demographic breakdowns, content performance, and strategic recommendations to grow the subscriber base and increase platform engagement.

---

## Contents

The presentation is structured into six sections:

1. **Executive Summary & KPIs** — Top-line performance figures for the quarter.
2. **User Profiles & Demographics** — Subscriber breakdown by age, gender, and geography.
3. **Viewership Trends & Usage Patterns** — Daily/weekly session volumes, day-of-week patterns, and a day × hour heatmap showing peak viewing windows (17:00–21:00 SAST).
4. **Factors Influencing Consumption** — Analysis of the key drivers: time of day, content type, demographics, geography, and live event spikes (e.g. ICC Cricket World Cup).
5. **Content Recommendations** — Strategies to lift low-engagement days (Mon–Wed) through targeted content: fitness/wellness, binge-series drops, eSports, documentaries, music countdowns, and local SA content.
6. **Growth Initiatives** — Six strategic initiatives to expand the subscriber base.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Friday evenings** are peak viewing — prioritise premium content in this window.
- **Sport & Music** are the two content pillars driving the most engagement.
- **Female and rural subscribers** represent the largest untapped growth segments.
- **Monday–Wednesday** require dedicated content strategies to lift midweek sessions.
- **Live events** (cricket, football) are powerful subscriber acquisition tools.
- A **referral + loyalty programme** can accelerate organic subscriber growth.
